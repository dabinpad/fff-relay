Scoring rules & points table
Trio = leg-1 runner (full) + average of the two leg-2 runners → t_leg1 + (a+b)/2. Fastest-on-leg-1 is set by default.
No-show (NS) — tick NS next to a runner in section 1 to mark them absent. They stay on their team as a marker and that team automatically becomes a capped no-show team (−5, max 6 pts); a present teammate runs the full 10 km. Optimise still finds the lowest-cost sacrifice (solo 2×5K, or a 10K-trio that buries a slow teammate).
Smart time entry — type digits and press Enter: 415→4:15, 2115→21:15, 4→4:00. In section 1 you can enter either the pace (5K fills in) or the 5K (pace fills in).
Clear all times — zeroes every pace/5K (keeping the lineups) so the board becomes a blank scorecard to fill in with the real race times.
DNF (toggle on a team) is placed last for 0 pts and takes an extra −5.
Actual times — both the team total (on each card) and each runner's leg time (next to their name) are editable. Editing the team total splits it proportionally across the runners; editing one runner's leg holds the team total and rebalances the partner — and the runner's pace updates to match.
Lock (toggle on a team) — pins that exact team: the optimiser won't touch it and its runners can't be added or removed (you can still edit times). E.g. put your chosen 10 km runner alone on a team and lock it, then optimise the rest.
🔒 Lock Blue / Lock Red (the big team lock) — pins that whole team: it ticks every one of that team's small-team locks, disables its optimise button, and keeps it fixed when you switch scenarios so only the other team changes. Lock your side, then cycle the opponent combos to compare scores.
Win % (row below the board) — a race-day Monte-Carlo win chance vs the opponent lineup currently on the board, over many races with each leg jittered ±10 s/km.
Tie on points → the team with the faster combined time wins.
